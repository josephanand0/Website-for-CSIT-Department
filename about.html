  • Department of Computer Science and Information Technology


    • True to its vision of “In pursuit of Excellence”, CVR College of Engineering was the first college in the State to introduce M.Tech programs in Artificial Intelligence and Data Sciences, well before AICTE could recognize them as emerging areas. Continuing the trend, CVR College pioneered a new B.Tech course in Computer Science and Information Technology (CSIT) in the year 2019, the brainchild of our Chairman. CVR was the only college in Telangana to do so at that time. The new course brings the best of both CSE and IT together providing CSIT graduates more opportunities than the graduates of CSE and IT courses. The emerging areas included in the new program have been identified by the national level committee as part of National Perspective Plan for Engineering Education and accepted by AICTE. Also, the courses provides employment and research opportunities in these areas.

    The program is designed with an intent to educate the engineering students on the emerging areas of Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), Internet of Things (IoT) and Data Sciences (DS) by introducing them as core courses. The program also educates the students with the related courses like statistics, R Programming, Big Data Analytics & Platforms, etc. Further, each student can choose from various streams in their professional electives that includes cutting edge technologies like Cyber Security (CS), Blockchain technologies, NoSQL Databases, Social Media Analytics, Neural Networks & Fuzzy Systems, Deep Learning and many more.

    The course of CSIT encompasses the core curriculum of CSE with specializations in the areas of AI, ML, DS, CS, IoT, etc. The college has recently introduced CSE (AI&ML), CSE (DS) and CSE (CS) courses that are more specialized in nature. If the prospective students are not yet sure of specializing in any of the above three areas, the CSIT course is a perfect blend of traditional CSE along with the three specializations.
    The Department has 3 laboratory units consisting of 90 systems, catering to the B. Tech curriculum. The systems use standard licensed software as a college policy including open source. Considering the department is only a year old and with only two batches of students the labs already cater to the entire four year program.
